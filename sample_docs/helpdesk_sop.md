# IT Helpdesk Standard Operating Procedure

## Ticket Severity and Response Times

| Severity | Description                       | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|----------|-----------------------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| 1        | Critical — production outage      | 15 minutes       | 4 hours           |
| 2        | High — major feature broken       | 1 hour           | 1 business day    |
| 3        | Medium — single user affected     | 4 hours          | 3 business days   |
| 4        | Low — cosmetic / enhancement      | 1 business day   | next sprint       |

All tickets are triaged by the on-duty helpdesk analyst within fifteen
(15) minutes of receipt during business hours.

## Ticket Lifecycle

Tickets move through these stages:

1. **New** — submitted by user, awaiting triage
2. **Triaged** — assigned a severity and owner
3. **In progress** — actively being worked on
4. **Pending user** — awaiting information or confirmation from requester
5. **Resolved** — fix applied, awaiting user confirmation
6. **Closed** — confirmed resolved

## Escalation

If a tier 1 helpdesk analyst cannot resolve a ticket, it is escalated
to tier 2 engineering. Severity 1 tickets that remain unresolved after
two (2) hours are automatically escalated to the on-call engineering
manager.

## Out of Scope

The IT helpdesk does not provide support for personal devices, personal
software licenses, or non-work-related issues.
